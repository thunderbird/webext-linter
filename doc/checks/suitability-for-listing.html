Check suitability for listing manual

suitability-for-listing

What it detects

This is a human-review item with no automated logic behind it. A reviewer judges who the add-on is actually meant for.

Some submissions target a limited or non-public audience — for example an internal tool, a single-organization add-on, or something built only for testing. Such add-ons are usually better self-hosted than published in the public ATN catalogue.

  classDef ok fill:#e9f5ee,stroke:#2c7a7b,color:#1d4d4d;

  class M manual;
When it fires The developer is told that, due to its limited or non-public audience, the add-on is not suitable for listing on ATN, and is asked to consider self-hosting it.

Source of truth: the manual-checks entry in assets/registry.yaml.
