Missing English localization (recheck) warning

missing-english-localization-recheck

What it detects

This is the post-summary recheck consumer for the deterministic missing-English-localization check. When that check cannot settle the language with confidence — too little text, or a near-tie with English — it escalates the case here instead of guessing.

During the --llm-review pass, that one item is appended to the add-on summary and re-judged by a model that reads all of the user-facing text together, which is far more reliable than language detection on a short sample. The model's verdict is then mapped to an ordinary outcome: a pass means the text is English or there is nothing to localize and the item is dropped; a fail becomes a missing-English-localization finding; an unsure verdict — or no verdict at all, when no summary ran — goes to manual review. This check does no language analysis of its own; it only consumes that verdict.

Decision flow

When it fires On a fail verdict, the add-on is asked to include an English localization, with the same guidance and i18n example as the producing check. Unsettled items are handed to a human reviewer instead.
