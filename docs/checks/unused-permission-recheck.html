Unused permission warning

unused-permission-recheck

What it detects

This is the post-summary recheck consumer for declared permissions. It does not enumerate anything itself: the unused-permission producer hands it every declared permission it could neither prove used nor deterministically prove unused (via the usage-token scan) and that the registry has a recheck prompt for, and the whole-add-on summary pass re-judges each one with full context.

Crucially, the producer does not just decide whether a permission’s tokens are present — it records where each occurs. A token-bearing permission is handed over with its list of token sites (file:line), and the summary judges each site: seeing the full, line-numbered add-on, is the permission actually exercised there? A token-less permission (such as unlimitedStorage, whose quota use has no reliable token) has no site to point at, so it gets one holistic verdict over the whole add-on.

resolvePermissionRecheck then aggregates the site verdicts per permission: if any site is exercised (pass) the permission is justified and dropped; if every site is definitively not (fail) it becomes a warning that the permission appears unused; anything else — a mix, all unsure, or no verdict at all when the summary was skipped or failed — routes the permission back to manual review. So one site’s answer never decides a whole permission alone, and a model that points a permission at concrete lines cannot wave it through on a bare yes/no. Because the orchestrator owns the sites, the model only judges them — there is no citation to hallucinate. With no summary nothing is handed over, so this check emits nothing and the producer’s reminder stands instead. The rubric it judges against is assembled per review from the registry’s permission-prompts, carrying only the sections for the permissions in play and the tabs wording that matches the add-on’s strict_min_version (the D308076 fix).

When it fires The add-on is told that a specific permission appears to be unused, and is asked to remove it or to say which part of the add-on stops working without it.

Source of truth: src/checks/rules/unused-permission-recheck.js and resolvePermissionRecheck (src/lib/recheck.js); the token sites come from enumerateUnusedPermissions / locateTokens (src/lib/permissions.js), and the rubric from the permission-prompts in assets/registry.yaml.
