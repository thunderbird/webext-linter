Unused permission warning

unused-permission-recheck

What it detects

This is the post-summary recheck consumer for declared permissions. It does not enumerate anything itself: the unused-permission producer hands it every declared permission it could neither prove used nor deterministically prove unused (via the usage-token scan) and that the registry has a recheck prompt for, and the whole-add-on summary pass re-judges each one with full context.

For each handed-over permission it maps the summary's verdict: a justified verdict drops the permission, an unused verdict becomes a warning that the permission appears unused, and an unsure verdict — or no verdict at all, when the summary was skipped or failed — routes the permission back to manual review. The model's per-permission reason rides along into the developer-facing wording. With no summary nothing is handed over, so this check emits nothing and the producer's reminder stands instead. The rubric it judges against is assembled per review from the registry's permission-prompts, carrying only the sections for the permissions in play and the tabs wording that matches the add-on's strict_min_version (the D308076 fix).

When it fires The add-on is told that a specific permission appears to be unused, with the summary's reason, and is asked to remove it or to say which part of the add-on stops working without it.
